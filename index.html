The problem

Public health information exists, but it is hard to turn into a decision.

Most people do not need more charts. They need fast context: what is happening near me, how serious is it, and what should I do today?

01

Alerts show up too late

By the time an update reaches social media or local news, people have already been making plans without clear information.

02

Headlines are too broad

National stories do not tell a student, commuter, or parent what the risk looks like in their own neighborhood.

03

Guidance feels generic

People need simple next steps based on their local conditions, not vague warnings that create stress without helping.

The solution

Vitrack turns scattered signals into one calm local picture.

The product brings together outbreak alerts, community trend shifts, and practical recommendations in one interface that feels useful in seconds.

Why it is different

Designed to feel trustworthy, readable, and useful in everyday life.

Vitrack is valuable because it is not trying to replace experts. It translates expert data into something regular people can actually use.

Neighborhood detail

Instead of giving a huge state-level summary, Vitrack focuses on the places where people actually move through their day.

Privacy-first by design
